Answer 2.1 (7p)¶

Loading model from file

               The CNN consists of five types of layers: Conv2D, MaxPooling2D, 
               Dropout, Dense and Flatten. It is divided into three segments 
               that progressively identity larger features.The first two segments 
               has two convolution filters, followed by a max pool and a drop out.
               The last segment has a dense layer in place of its max pool layer, 
               a flatten layer before it to transform from 2D domain back to 
               1D domain.The three dropout layers regularizes each level of detail.
               The size of the dense layer, 32 nodes, is chosen because this is 
               the size after flattening the last convolutional layer. Finally, 
               the dropout increases from 0.2 to 0.5 across the stages as the 
               final stage has the most direct impact on the model prediction.
               Also, earlier layers are affected multiple times by dropout at 
               later layers, but not the other way around.
